I want to go home, I’ve spent the majority of my life in this city
When I say I want to leave, I’m not talking about the good vibes
I am talking about the people staring intently into space as if contemplating the universe for hours
I am talking about the person sitting silently at the sill of a 10 foot window
Who never opens his or her eyes And I’m not talking about the park benches
That make you fall over Like park benches do,
I’m talking about the stooped slits in the leather
Like these eyes, they dart from one end of the sidewalk to the other Like these legs, they run like a marathon, almost too fast for comfort
And I’m not talking about the music I am talking about the people walking hand in hand, long after the sun has gone
